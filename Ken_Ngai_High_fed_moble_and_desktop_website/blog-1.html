Stanley Park Seawall

Stanley Park Seawall is in the downtown core of Vancouver. The Stanley Park Seawall Greenway loops around Stanley Park, providing a ride that is about 9.5 km long. The Greenway runs along Seawall, hugging the water and offering endless of sightseeing point to cyclists.

  • Road: All paved
  • Difficulty level: Easy - Middle
  • Safety level: Safe for the whole family
  • Suitable for: Everyone

Follow the map below and check out some of the sightseeing point!


The 9 O’Clock Gun

The 9 O’Clock Gun is a cannon that is shot every night at 9 pm. The cannon is activated automatically with an electronic trigger, and it acts as sightseeing point in Stanley Park.

Lions Gate Bridge

The Lions Gate Bridge is the first bridge built from Vancouver to North Vancouver. The name "Lions Gate" refers to The Lions, a pair of mountain peaks north of Vancouver. The road to the bridge goes directly through the Stanley Park.

Totem Poles

The nine totem poles are BC’s most visited tourist attraction, the totem poles each have a unique history.

Girl in a Wetsuit

“Girl in a Wetsuit” is a life-size bronze sculpture by Elek Imredy and remains as a landmark of Stanley Park.

The SS Empress of Japan Figurehead

The SS Empress of Japan Figurehead is a colourful replica of the actual Figurehead of the ship that crossed the Pacific Ocean over 400 times to do trading between Vancouver and the East in the 1890’s.

Beaver Lake

Beavers are living in this very shallow lake and if you carefully can see the beaver lodge, which looks like a small island from afar.

Siwash Rock

The wonders of nature create some of the greatest geological sights. The Siwash Rock is a 15 meter high famous rock outcropping in the seaside of Stanley Park.

The Lost Lagoon

The lake features a lit fountain and the name came from the leading local poet Pauline Johnson to write “Ode to the Lost Lagoon.” It is also a nesting ground for many species of birds, including mute swan, Canada geese, and numerous species of ducks.

Third Beach

This is a hot spot for cyclist to lock your bike and chill at the beach! The beach also offers great views of Kitsilano and the North Shore.
